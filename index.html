Welcome to Open Laser Metal Deposition

OpenLMD (Open Laser Metal Deposition) is a set of software components which define a modular and open architecture to demonstrate last advances on laser processing monitoring and control systems. Built on ROS (Robot Operating System), the modular approach provided in OpenLMD pursues the deployment of new algorithms beyond the state-of-the-art in real industrial facilities, fixing common interoperability and standarization issues.

Focus on Laser Metal Deposition (LMD) to build and repair large and complex metallic parts, it takes advantage from open source and from most advanced robotic, vision, and machine learning research towards a zero defects manufacturing.

OpenLMD was born from AIMEN's development efforts on several projects, mainly driven by the MAShES project, and PROPER and CyPLAM assesments from LASHARE and FORTISSIMO 2.

Introduction

Laser Metal Deposition (LMD) is an additive laser deposition technique focused on direct manufacturing of metal parts, through the successive deposition of melted tracks layer-by-layer, building the component directly from its CAD model.

Unlike Selective Laser Melting (SLM), consisting on the deposition of powder layers and their subsequent laser melt (mainly used for rapid prototyping), LMD only adds the required material.

This Additive Manufacturing (AM) technique works on current processing technology, known as Laser Cladding, commonly used for repair of metal components. This makes LMD a promising technique for retrofitting of current industrial facilities towards the digital factory (Industrie 4.0).
