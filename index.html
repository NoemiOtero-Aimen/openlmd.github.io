Welcome to Open Laser Metal Deposition

OpenLMD (Open Laser Metal Deposition) is a set of software components provided to demonstrate last advances on laser processing control systems. Built on ROS (Robot Operating System), the modular approach provided in OpenLMD pursues the deployment of new algorithms beyond the state-of-the-art in real facilities, fixing common interoperability and standarization issues. Moreover, it takes advantage from most advanced robotic, vision, and machine learning research open source.

OpenLMD is born from AIMEN's development efforts on several projects, mainly driven by the MAShES project, the PROPER assesment from the LASHARE project and the CyPLAM experiment from the FORTISSIMO2 project.

Introduction

Laser Metal Deposition (LMD) is an additive laser deposition technique focused on direct manufacturing of metal parts, through the successive deposition of melted tracks layer by layer, building the component directly from its CAD model.

Unlike Selective Laser Melting (SLM), consisting on the deposition of powder layers and their subsequent laser melt (mainly used for rapid prototyping), LMD only adds the required material.

This additive manufacturing (AM) technique works on current processing technology, known as Laser Cladding, commonly used for repair of metal components. This makes LMD a promising technique for retrofitting of current industrial facilities towards the digital factory (Industrie 4.0).

Subprojects

MAShES aims to develop a multimodal embeded imaging system for RT close-loop control of LMD systems.

PROPER aims to develop an 3D geometrical control of LMD for an automatic repair of large and comple parts.

CyPLAM aims to develop an adaptive parametric control of LMD systems based on Big Data and Deep Learning.
